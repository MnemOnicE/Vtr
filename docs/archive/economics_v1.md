# [ARCHIVED] The Economic Model: DePIN & Net Metering

> **DEPRECATION NOTICE:**
> This document has been archived as of VTR V2.2. The project focus has shifted exclusively to providing a robust hardware-attestation standard for security and verification. The economic models described below (DePIN, Tokens, Net Metering) are preserved here for historical context only and are **not** part of the active VTR standard.

## 1. Overview
The `.vtr` ecosystem is designed as a Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Network (DePIN) that incentivizes the creation of verified, human-generated data. It employs a "Net Metering" model where users are compensated for the data they generate, effectively allowing their phones to pay for their own data plans.

## 2. The Asset: Certified Organic Data
As AI models consume the internet, synthetic data pollution ("Model Collapse") becomes a critical risk. `.vtr` files represent a scarce and valuable resource: **Verified Human Reality**. This data is essential for training robust AI models and monitoring physical infrastructure.

## 3. The "Invisible Wallet" Architecture
To ensure mass adoption, the economic layer abstracts away the complexities of cryptocurrency.

### 3.1. Utility Token ($VTR)
*   A background utility token ($VTR) serves as the settlement unit within the network.
*   Tokens are minted when a device generates valid, verified data (Proof of Reality).

### 3.2. Net Metering Experience
*   **No Crypto Wallets:** Users do not need to manage private keys or exchange accounts.
*   **Fiat Conversion:** The "Data Trust" (DAO) acts as an intermediary. It sells the pooled data to buyers and converts the $VTR tokens into fiat currency.
*   **Direct Bill Credit:** The resulting value is applied directly as a credit to the user's mobile carrier bill.

## 4. Market Dynamics

### 4.1. The Sellers (Users)
*   **Incentive:** Users are financially rewarded for recording reality.
*   **Equity:** The "Potato Phone" (budget Android) is a first-class citizen. High-noise sensors provide high-quality verification, democratizing access to the economy.

### 4.2. The Buyers
*   **AI Companies:** Purchase "Blanket Licenses" to access the "Human Data Pool" for model training, ensuring compliance and data integrity.
*   **Municipalities & Insurance:** Purchase data for infrastructure monitoring (e.g., pothole detection, accident verification).

## 5. Economic Stability
*   **Data Trust (DAO):** A non-profit foundation governs the protocol, ensuring no single entity captures more than 15% of the voting power.
*   **Semantic Score Caps:** To prevent perverse incentives (e.g., creating chaos to record it), rewards are capped based on semantic value, ensuring a "Street Fight" earns no more than a "Traffic Jam."
